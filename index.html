About

The Annual Percentage Rate (APR) on a credit card is the interest rate charged on any unpaid balance on the card. The APR calculation typically takes into account the card's interest rate, any fees or charges associated with the card, and the frequency of compounding. Credit card companies are required by law to disclose the APR clearly, so that consumers can make informed decisions about using credit cards.

Mortgage rates are the interest rates charged by lenders on loans for home purchases or refinancing. These rates are influenced by various factors such as the current market conditions, the borrower's credit score and financial situation, and the type and term of the mortgage. The final mortgage rate can also include additional fees or points paid by the borrower to lower the interest rate.

A CD (Certificate of Deposit) and a savings account are both types of deposit accounts offered by banks and credit unions. The key difference between them is CDs typically offer a higher interest rate but require the depositor to commit to leaving the funds in the account for a specific term. Savings accounts, on the other hand, are more flexible but generally offer lower interest rates than CDs.
